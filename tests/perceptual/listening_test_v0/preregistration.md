## Study Overview

**Participants**: N = 12 listeners (normal hearing, 18–40 years, no prior experience with
the specific stimuli)
**Venue**: SNU MARG listening room (treated, RT60 < 200 ms)
**Equipment**: Lab headphone rig for binaural; lab_8ch / lab_4ch speaker arrays for
loudspeaker conditions.

---

## Research Questions

1. **RQ1**: Does VBAP produce lower perceived azimuth error than DBAP on the lab_8ch layout?
2. **RQ2**: Does lab_8ch produce lower perceived azimuth error than lab_4ch under VBAP?

---

## Stimulus Pipeline

Stimuli generated by **vid2spatial v3** at `/home/seung/mmhoa/vid2spatial_v2`.

- Input: 30-second video clips (N=8 clips, covering all four quadrants).
- Output: multichannel audio rendered to lab_8ch or lab_4ch speaker layout.
- Rendering algorithms: VBAP and DBAP (v0 engine, no post-processing).
- Level normalization: ITU-R BS.1770-4, -23 LUFS.
- Clip order: randomized per listener via Latin square (8 clips × 4 conditions = 32 trials).

## Primary Friedman Contrasts

**(a) VBAP vs DBAP on lab_8ch** (conditions A vs B)

- DV: perceived azimuth error (degrees), per direction, per listener.
- Test: Friedman χ² across conditions A and B, within-listener.
- H0: no difference in azimuth error distribution between VBAP and DBAP.

**(b) lab_4ch vs lab_8ch on VBAP** (conditions C vs A)

- DV: perceived azimuth error (degrees), per direction, per listener.
- Test: Friedman χ² across conditions C and A, within-listener.
- H0: no difference in azimuth error distribution between 4ch and 8ch layouts.

---

## Per-Direction Criterion Threshold (Acceptance A3)

For each measured direction, the study passes if:

- Mean perceived azimuth error ≤ **1°** AND
- 95% confidence interval upper bound ≤ **2°**

This applies to conditions A and B (lab_8ch). Condition C (lab_4ch) is measured for
comparison but has a relaxed threshold of mean ≤ 3°.

---

## Analysis Plan

1. Collect raw responses: listener-reported azimuth estimate per stimulus per trial.
2. Compute error: `error_deg = reported_az - actual_az` (signed).
3. Per direction: compute mean, SD, 95% CI (bootstrap, 10 000 resamples).
4. Primary tests: Friedman χ² on perceived-azimuth-error distributions for contrasts (a) and (b).
5. Pairwise: Wilcoxon signed-rank test for each direction pair where Friedman is significant.
6. Multiple comparisons: Bonferroni correction across directions within each contrast.
7. Report: effect size (Kendall W for Friedman), r for Wilcoxon.

All analysis scripts will be committed to `tests/perceptual/listening_test_v0/analysis/`
before data collection begins.

---

## Exclusion Criteria

- Listener excluded if: reported azimuth error > 2 SD from group mean on **any single direction**.
- Excluded listeners are replaced (replacement pre-registered: up to N+3 = 15 total recruited).
- Exclusion decisions are made blind to condition labels (analysis assistant who does not
  know the condition-stimulus mapping applies the criterion).
